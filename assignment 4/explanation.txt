The above video is of Minecraft rain. I always loved the effect in-game and thought it'd be fun to try and make it myself.
I made the rain blue to match and made sure to keep the alpha value pretty low. I added a crosswind slider to add an angle
to the rain. The rain moves faster with a higher angle to match the visual illusion in real life, though I don't think 
Minecraft rain has a crosswind in it. The hardest part was adding the bounce along the bottom of the screen. I had to increase
the amount of parameters and got a really annoying bug because the Particle struct was 2 vec2fs and an f32, but the GPU
needs the parameters to be a multiple of the largest data structure in the Particle struct (I think), so I had to add a padding
f32 to it. The extra data acts like a boolean to tell the compute shader if the particle is a droplet or a rain particle at
that moment. When the rain particle gets to the bottom of the screen it gets a random very small x and a small y velocity.
At the next pass the now droplet particle has gravity enacted on it until it falls back off the screen and the cycle repeats.
It makes a fun effect along the bottom of the screen, but it does not work properly with the crosswind effect, but that's fine.
